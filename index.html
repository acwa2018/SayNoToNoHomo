FADA

FADA (First Amendment Defense Act) is an act of legislation that gives organizations and business the right to not surve people who are homosexual based on their religious beliefs. It was first brought to the house by representitive Raul Labrador in 2015. It was not passed and died in the house but recently it has been getting more attention because of President Donald Trumps attorney general pick Jeff Sessions. Sessions a senator supported the bill and this support has been a reason for concern for many groups that supoort LGBT rights. As attorney general Sessions job is to enforce the law and act as the "People's lawyer" for all citizens.

Harvey Milk

A person who is known for pioneering for gay rights in the realm of politics is a Mr. Harvey Milk. Harvey Milk was the first openly gay politician to be elected to political office. He was elected to the San Fransico Board of Supervisors in 1978. He was born in New York and moved to California in his late 30's. His assasination led to people really rethinking how we treat about gay people in this nation. Harvey Milk will go down as one of the best and first leaders of the gay community.

"NO HOMO BRO!"

If so, you might recognize situations that are similar to this one where someone says "no homo" for no reason, but to show their manefestation of insecurity involving their sexuality.
